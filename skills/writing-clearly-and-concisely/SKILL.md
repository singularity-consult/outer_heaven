# writing-clearly-and-concisely

Clear, concise prose. The rules come from Strunk's *The Elements of Style*. They apply to any prose a person will read: docs, commit messages, PR descriptions, error messages, UI copy, code comments. The principles transfer to Danish even though the book is English.

## The core rules

- **Omit needless words.** Every word should do work. Cut "in order to" to "to", "due to the fact that" to "because", "at this point in time" to "now". Vigorous writing is concise.
- **Use the active voice.** "The hook injects the profile", not "the profile is injected by the hook". Active is shorter and clearer about who does what.
- **Put statements in positive form.** Say what is, not what isn't. "forgot" beats "did not remember"; "ignored" beats "did not pay attention to".
- **Use definite, specific, concrete language.** Prefer the particular to the vague. "Push failed with 403" beats "something went wrong with the push".
- **Use parallel structure for parallel ideas.** A list of three should read as three of the same grammatical shape.
- **Keep related words together.** Subject near its verb, modifiers near what they modify. Avoid sentences that make the reader hold their breath.
- **Do not overstate.** Overstatement makes the reader distrust everything else. State what is true and let it stand.
- **Cut qualifiers that weaken.** "rather", "very", "little", "pretty" usually dilute. Remove them or pick a stronger word.

## How to apply it

This is a revising rule, not only a writing rule. Write the sentence, then cut. The second draft of a sentence is usually half the length of the first and says more.

For commit messages and error messages especially, a reader skimming under pressure benefits most from concision. Lead with the point. One clear sentence beats three hedged ones.
